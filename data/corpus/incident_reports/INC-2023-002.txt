Reported To: Kavitha Reddy (EMP-007), Safety Officer

INCIDENT DESCRIPTION
--------------------
During routine tank farm inspection, Shift Supervisor Sunita Patel noticed the gauge hatch on T-401 was not properly seated. Slight hydrocarbon vapor odor detected in the immediate vicinity.

Investigation revealed:
- Gauge hatch gasket was worn and not sealing properly
- Condition had likely existed for several days
- No visible liquid leak, but vapor emissions occurring

IMMEDIATE CAUSE
---------------
Worn gauge hatch gasket allowing vapor emissions.

UNDERLYING FACTORS
------------------
1. Gasket was last replaced during tank inspection in 2021
2. No scheduled inspection interval for tank hatch gaskets in current PM program
3. Gasket material may not be suitable for naphtha service

POTENTIAL CONSEQUENCES (IF UNDETECTED)
--------------------------------------
- Continuous VOC emissions contributing to air quality issues
- Environmental non-compliance risk
- Potential fire hazard from vapor accumulation in enclosed areas

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
------------------
Immediate:
- Gauge hatch gasket replaced immediately
- T-401 area monitored with portable gas detector - clear

Short-term:
- All tank gauge hatches inspected - two additional worn gaskets found and replaced
- Added gauge hatch gasket inspection to annual tank inspection checklist

LESSONS LEARNED
---------------
1. Visual inspection of tank fittings should be included in routine operator rounds
2. Gasket replacement should be scheduled, not just reactive
