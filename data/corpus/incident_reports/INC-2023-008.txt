INCIDENT DESCRIPTION
--------------------
Significant hydrocarbon leak from P-101A mechanical seal detected by operator during rounds. Leak rate was substantial - visible spray and pooling of product observed. Strong hydrocarbon odor in the area.

Initial gas detector reading: LEL 15% in immediate vicinity of pump.

Emergency response initiated:
1. Emergency alarm raised
2. Area evacuation within 3 minutes
3. Fire crew deployed on standby
4. ESD activation for P-101A
5. Standby pump P-101B brought online from control room

IMMEDIATE CAUSE
---------------
Mechanical seal failure - complete loss of sealing function. Seal faces damaged beyond repair.

UNDERLYING FACTORS
------------------
1. This is the second seal failure on P-101A in 6 months (first failure: WO-2023-003, March 2023)
2. Seal elastomers appeared swollen and degraded upon removal
3. Lubricant in bearing housing: EconoLube Standard 320 (last changed in WO-2023-010, July 2023)

POTENTIAL CONSEQUENCES (IF UNDETECTED)
--------------------------------------
- Major hydrocarbon release with potential for fire/explosion
- LEL readings already at 15% - approaching dangerous levels
- Multiple personnel work in the area during day shift
- If incident had occurred during night shift with reduced manning, delayed detection could have resulted in serious consequences

RISK ASSESSMENT
---------------
Without intervention:
- Probability of ignition: MEDIUM (electrical equipment in area)
- Potential consequence: MAJOR (fire, personnel injury, equipment damage)
- Risk rating: HIGH

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS (PRELIMINARY)
---------------------------------
Root cause: UNDER INVESTIGATION

Observations:
1. Seal elastomers showed swelling - possible chemical exposure
2. Pump ran without seal issues from 2018 to early 2023
3. Two failures in 6 months is unusual for this equipment

Maintenance engineering to review service history and recent changes. Failed seal and lubricant samples to be sent for laboratory analysis.

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
------------------
Immediate:
- Seal replaced under WO-2023-013
- Area gas-freed and returned to normal operations
- Failed seal preserved for analysis

Short-term:
- Send lubricant and failed seal samples to external laboratory
- Review recent maintenance records for any changes in materials or practices

LESSONS LEARNED
---------------
1. Two similar failures on same equipment warrants investigation
2. Emergency response was effective - regular drills pay off
3. Preserving failed components for analysis is important
