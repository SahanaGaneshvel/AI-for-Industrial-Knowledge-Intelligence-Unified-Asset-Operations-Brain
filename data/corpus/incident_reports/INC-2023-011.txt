INCIDENT / NEAR-MISS REPORT

Report Number: INC-2023-011
Date of Incident: 2023-10-22
Time: 11:30 hours
Classification: NEAR-MISS
Severity: LOW

LOCATION
--------
Area: Compressor House
Specific Location: C-102 Hydrogen Recycle Compressor

PERSONNEL INVOLVED
------------------
Discovered By: Rajesh Kumar (EMP-001), Senior Maintenance Engineer
During: Routine vibration monitoring rounds

INCIDENT DESCRIPTION
--------------------
During scheduled vibration data collection, Maintenance Engineer Rajesh Kumar noticed elevated vibration readings on C-102 compressor. Readings were trending upward over past 3 data collection cycles but had not triggered alarm.

Trend analysis:
- August 2023: 2.1 mm/s (baseline)
- September 2023: 2.8 mm/s
- October 2023: 3.9 mm/s (near alarm threshold of 4.5 mm/s)

Proactive investigation identified loosened foundation anchor bolts.

IMMEDIATE CAUSE
---------------
Foundation bolt loosening causing structural vibration amplification.

UNDERLYING FACTORS
------------------
1. High-cycle fatigue on foundation bolts
2. Insufficient torque verification in PM procedures
3. Vibration trend analysis not reviewed regularly

POTENTIAL CONSEQUENCES (IF UNDETECTED)
--------------------------------------
- Continued vibration increase leading to bearing damage
- Potential compressor trip on high vibration
- Possible foundation cracking if left uncorrected
- Unplanned shutdown of hydrogen recycle system

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
------------------
Immediate:
- Foundation bolts retorqued to specification (WO-2023-004)
- Post-repair vibration: 1.9 mm/s (below baseline)

Short-term:
- Added foundation bolt torque check to quarterly PM procedure
- Implemented automated vibration trending alerts

LESSONS LEARNED
---------------
1. Trending data is valuable for early problem detection
2. Foundation integrity should be part of rotating equipment PM program
3. Proactive maintenance prevents reactive failures
