INCIDENT DESCRIPTION
--------------------
Major mechanical seal failure on P-101A with significant hydrocarbon release. Control room received seal failure alarm at 09:15 hours. Operator dispatched to investigate found substantial leak with pooling product.

Emergency Shutdown System (ESD) activated from control room.

Leak rate estimated: 2 liters/minute (major release)
Total estimated release: ~50 liters before isolation complete
LEL reading: 25% peak, requiring full area evacuation

Emergency response timeline:
- 09:15 - Alarm received in control room
- 09:17 - Operator on scene, confirms major leak
- 09:18 - ESD activated, emergency alarm raised
- 09:20 - Area evacuation complete
- 09:25 - Fire crew on station
- 09:45 - LEL readings dropping below 10%
- 10:30 - Area declared safe for maintenance entry
- 13:15 - Seal replacement complete
- 14:00 - Pump returned to service with extended monitoring

No injuries reported. No fire or ignition.

IMMEDIATE CAUSE
---------------
Catastrophic mechanical seal failure. Seal faces damaged, elastomers showed severe degradation.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
-------------------
Status: UNDER INVESTIGATION

Preliminary findings from seal autopsy:
- Elastomer components (FFKM O-rings) showed chemical degradation
- Swelling pattern suggests exposure to incompatible substance
- Lubricant sample collected and sent to external lab (Report #LAB-2024-0142 pending)

Reliability Engineering has been tasked with formal RCA. This is the third P-101A seal failure in the past 12 months - an unusual frequency that warrants investigation.

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS TAKEN
------------------------
Immediate:
1. Seal replaced
2. Bearing housing flushed and refilled with VantaLube Premium 320
3. Enhanced monitoring schedule implemented

Pending Investigation:
1. Await lab analysis results
2. Review maintenance history for changes in practices or materials
3. Formal RCA to be completed within 30 days

LESSONS LEARNED (PRELIMINARY)
-----------------------------
1. Multiple similar failures on same equipment should trigger earlier investigation
2. Emergency response was effective - drills and training paid off
3. Importance of preserving failed components for analysis

REGULATORY NOTIFICATION

Not required - no injuries, release contained within plant boundary, below reportable threshold.
